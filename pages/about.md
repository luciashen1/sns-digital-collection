## About the Scotch'n'Soda Theatre Digital Collection
The Scotch'n'Soda Digital Collection exists as an online repository of programs, posters, and various other ephemera from each mainstage show the organization has put on. It currently ranges from 1938 - 1998, and intends to expand to include shows up until the present day and serve as a living archive of the longstanding theatre group. The digital collections are maintained by current members of Scotch'n'Soda in partnership with the Carnegie Mellon University Archives. 

The digitized collection began as an initiative of Lucia Shen, a 2026 Fifth Year Scholar at Carnegie Mellon, as part of their community impact project. It is currently maintained by current members of Scotch'n'Soda in partnership with the Carnegie Mellon University Archives. 

## About Scotch'n'Soda Theatre

Scotch'n'Soda Theatre, the student-run theatre organization of Carnegie Mellon University, is the oldest coed student theatre organization in the U.S. The club has a number of notable alumni and works such as Stephen Schwartz's Pippin, Pippin, which was written and produced for the Scotch'n'Soda 1967 Carnival slot. The show was staged as Pippin on Broadway five years later, and Stephen Schwartz would also go on to write Wicked and Godspell.

Scotch'n'Soda Theatre was originally founded in 1907 as the White Friars Club, but after a hiatus from World War II, the troupe returned as Scotch'n'Soda Theatre in 1937. The organization started out by producing completely original works by students for Carnegie Mellon's Carnival festivities, beginning with No Strings in 1938. In the 1960s, Scotch'n'Soda began producing shows for Homecoming in addition to Carnival. Currently, the organization puts on four to five mainstage shows a year in addition to supporting smaller theatrical projects on a year by year basis.
